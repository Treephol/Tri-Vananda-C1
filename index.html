Residential Villas

Private pool villas built for
multigenerational living.
Two styles. Seven layouts.

The residential villas at Tri Vananda are fully furnished and equipped in two, three, and four-bedroom configurations. Two villa styles — Vana, a biophilic design rooted in timber, courtyards, and forest; and Nanda, a modernist expression of curved glass, light, and eco-conscious architecture. Residents choose their location within the community first, then select a layout from seven options spanning 325 to 780 sq.m.

Every villa includes a private swimming pool, full front pool deck, and en suite bathrooms. Ceiling heights range from 4 to 6.7 metres. The largest residences feature Asian kitchens, maid quarters, second-floor living areas, and 20-metre pools. All villas are fully furnished and equipped, delivered complete and ready to live in.

Community House & The Farm

Where the community gathers.
Where the food is grown.
Where family belong.

The Community House is the social centre of Tri Vananda — a swimming pool, an Active Movement Studio, a Tweenie Club designed for younger residents to learn, play, and connect, and the shared spaces where families come together naturally.

At its heart is Jampa, a Michelin Green Star restaurant built around local ingredients, live-fire cooking, and zero-waste cuisine. Everything is grown at The Farm at Tri Vananda, sourced from local fishing communities, or transformed from what would otherwise be discarded. The regenerative farm is not decoration — it is infrastructure. Soil that improves rather than depletes. A closed-loop system that feeds the restaurant, supplies the community, and returns more to the land than it takes.

Nature

232 acres of abundant
biodiversity. Not a setting.
The foundation.

Only 15% of the land is built on. The rest — nine lakes, forest, and a wetland nature reserve — is a living ecosystem teeming with native wildlife, migratory birds, and hundreds of plant species.

Nature here was not installed. It was always here — we simply chose to preserve it. The air is shaped by the canopy. The water is filtered through tiered wetlands. The biodiversity grows richer with each season.

This is land your grandchildren will inherit — not just in title, but in memory. The forest they climbed. The lake they swam. The place that shaped how they see the world.
